### 🔍 Damage Types to Inspect For

**Roof System**
- Bruised, cracked, or missing asphalt shingles
- Dented or displaced metal roofing panels
- Damaged ridge vents, pipe boots, or chimney flashing
- Granule loss visible in gutters or downspouts
- Damaged soffit/fascia boards

**Exterior Systems**
- Dented, cracked, or torn vinyl/aluminum siding
- Damaged gutters, downspouts, or gutter guards
- Broken, cracked, or fogged windows
- Damaged garage doors, entry doors, or shutters
- Displaced or damaged fascia, soffit, or trim

**Other Structures**
- Damaged fences, gates, or retaining walls
- Hail damage to vehicles (if comprehensive coverage applies)
- Destroyed or damaged landscaping, trees, or shrubs
- Damaged HVAC condenser coils or exterior units

---

### 📋 Why You Need a Professional Inspection

Even if you do not see obvious damage from the ground, **hail and wind damage is often hidden** and may not cause leaks for months or years. By then, the statute of limitations for filing a claim may have expired.

Under **215 ILCS 5/154.6**, Illinois law requires insurers to provide reasonable claim investigation, and under **215 ILCS 5/155**, insurers who unreasonably delay or deny claims may be liable for damages, attorney fees, and costs.
