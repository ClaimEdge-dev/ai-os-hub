### Standards of Care for Insurance Inspectors

1. **Independent Judgment:** The inspector must exercise independent professional judgment without influence from the carrier, policyholder, or contractor.
2. **Competence:** Inspectors must maintain current certifications (Haag, state license) and only inspect property types within their competency.
3. **Thoroughness:** All accessible areas must be inspected. If areas are inaccessible, this must be documented with the reason.
4. **Documentation:** All findings must be documented with photographs, measurements, and written descriptions. Memory alone is insufficient.
5. **Objectivity:** The inspector must report what is observed, regardless of which party the findings may favor.
6. **Safety:** No inspection finding is worth risking personal safety. Inspectors must stop work when conditions are unsafe.
7. **Continuity:** The same inspector should perform initial inspection and any re-inspections to maintain consistency.
8. **Timeliness:** Reports must be delivered within the timeframe promised to the client, typically 24-48 hours for residential, 48-72 hours for commercial.
