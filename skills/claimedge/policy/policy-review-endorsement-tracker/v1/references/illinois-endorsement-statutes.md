## Primary Statutes

### 215 ILCS 5/143.1 — Changes in Policy Terms

**Text:**
> "No policy of insurance ... shall be issued or delivered ... unless any changes in
> the terms of the policy are ... conspicuously stated in the policy or in a rider or
> endorsement attached to and made a part of the policy."

**Application to Endorsement Tracking:**
- Requires all coverage-reducing changes to be "conspicuous"
- Changes must be in a rider or endorsement "attached to and made a part of" the policy
- Creates private right of action for insured when carrier fails to comply
- Burden of proof on carrier to demonstrate conspicuousness

**Key Points:**
- "Conspicuous" means presented so a reasonable person would notice it
- Applies to all policy changes, not just endorsements
- Violation renders the change unenforceable against the insured

### 215 ILCS 5/154.6 — Unfair Claims Settlement Practices

**Text (relevant subsections):**

**(d)(1)** — Misrepresenting pertinent facts or insurance policy provisions relating to coverages at issue

**(d)(7)** — Failing to adopt and implement reasonable standards for the prompt investigation of claims arising under insurance policies

**(d)(8)** — Failing to acknowledge and act reasonably promptly upon communications with respect to claims arising under insurance policies

**(d)(14)** — Failing to provide promptly to an insured a reasonable explanation of the basis in the insurance policy for the denial of a claim or offer of a compromise settlement

**Application to Endorsement Tracking:**
- Adding restrictive endorsements mid-claim to avoid payment = misrepresentation (d)(1)
- Delaying claim to add endorsement first = failing prompt investigation (d)(7)
- Not explaining basis for reliance on new endorsement = violation of (d)(14)
- Pattern of mid-claim endorsements across multiple claims = systematic unfair practice

**Remedy:** IDOI complaint + potential private right of action for actual damages.

---

### 215 ILCS 5/155 — Bad Faith

**Text:**
> "In any action ... where it is found that the insurance company ... has refused to
> pay the full amount of a claim without reasonable cause, the court may enter
> judgment for ... attorney's fees, prejudgment interest, and punitive damages."

**Application to Endorsement Tracking:**
- Deliberately adding endorsements mid-claim to avoid payment = unreasonable refusal
- Pattern of conduct across multiple claims = strong bad faith evidence
- Attorney fees provision makes this economically viable for insureds
- Prejudgment interest (9% in Illinois) adds significant exposure for carrier

**Key Difference from 5/154.6:**
- 5/154.6: Regulatory violation (IDOI complaint)
- 5/155: Private cause of action with fee-shifting and punitive damages
- Best practice: Plead both; use 5/154.6 for regulatory pressure, 5/155 for litigation

---

### 215 ILCS 5/154 — Policy Cancellation and Nonrenewal

**Text:**
Requires specific notice periods for policy cancellation (typically 30 days) and
nonrenewal (typically 60 days). Mid-term coverage reductions require similar notice.

**Application:**
- Endorsement that effectively cancels coverage may trigger cancellation notice requirements
- If carrier did not provide proper notice period, endorsement is void
- Useful argument when endorsement is functionally equivalent to cancellation

---

### 815 ILCS 505/1 — Consumer Fraud and Deceptive Business Practices Act

**Text:**
Prohibits unfair methods of competition and deceptive acts in commerce.

**Application:**
- Adding coverage restrictions without disclosure = deceptive practice
- Pattern of mid-claim endorsements = unfair method of competition
- Available even when insurance-specific remedies are insufficient
- Treble damages available under 815 ILCS 505/10a

## Administrative Regulations

### 50 Ill. Adm. Code Part 919 — Standards for Policy Forms

**919.20 — Policy Changes:**
> "Any change in policy terms, conditions, or coverage that reduces benefits or
> increases exclusions must be presented in a manner that is clear and conspicuous
to the insured."

**919.30 — Notice Requirements:**
> "Insurers must provide written notice of any policy change to the insured at least
> 30 days before the effective date of the change, unless the change is at the
> request of the insured."

**Application:**
- Endorsements added without 30-day advance notice violate 919.30
- "At the request of the insured" exception rarely applies to restrictive changes
- IDOI can sanction carrier for 919 violations

---

### 50 Ill. Adm. Code Part 3902 — Market Conduct Examinations

IDOI may conduct market conduct examinations to determine if carriers are
systematically adding restrictive endorsements mid-claim. Pattern evidence across
multiple claims can trigger regulatory scrutiny.

## Case Law by Category

### Coverage Interpretation — Endorsement Effective Dates

| Case | Citation | Key Holding |
|------|----------|-------------|
| *Sullivan v. 21st Century* | 2015 IL App (1st) | Policy terms as of date of loss control; subsequent changes inapplicable |
| *Poole v. N. River Ins. Co.* | 1990 WL 138124 | Post-loss endorsement cannot defeat coverage for occurred event |
| *Maryland Cas. Co. v. Peppers* | 64 Ill. App. 3d 435 | Coverage fixed at time of occurrence; later endorsements irrelevant |
| *Cincinnati Ins. Co. v. H.D. Smith* | 829 F.3d 771 (7th Cir. 2016) | Endorsement ineffective without proper notice; burden on insurer |
| *Rhodes v. Chicago Ins. Co.* | 231 Ill. App. 3d 538 | Insurer must prove insured received and understood endorsement |
| *S & S Fitting Corp. v. Zurich* | 2014 IL App (1st) 130512 | Conspicuous notice required for coverage changes; burden on insurer |

### Bad Faith — Mid-Claim Conduct

| Case | Citation | Key Holding |
|------|----------|-------------|
| *Cramer v. Ins. Exch. Agency* | 675 N.E.2d 1038 | Post-claim coverage reduction supports bad faith finding |
| *Am. Family Mut. Ins. Co. v. Savickas* | 193 Ill. 2d 378 | Pattern of claim denial tactics = bad faith evidence |
| *Aqua Pip Co. v. Gt. Am. Ins. Co.* | 2018 IL App (1st) | Unreasonable delay + shifting positions = bad faith |

### Notice and Conspicuousness

| Case | Citation | Key Holding |
|------|----------|-------------|
| *Partridge v. Bankers Life* | 2016 IL App (4th) | Burial of coverage change in renewal packet = insufficient notice |
| *Grove Mgmt. v. Am. Motorists Ins.* | 212 Ill. App. 3d 853 | Agent knowledge of change does not equal insured knowledge |
| *Davis v. Allstate* | 2017 IL App (1st) | Online portal posting without direct notification insufficient |

## Statute of Limitations

| Claim Type | SOL | Statute | Notes |
|------------|-----|---------|-------|
| Breach of contract (policy claim) | 10 years | 735 ILCS 5/13-206 | From date of breach (denial) |
| Bad faith (215 ILCS 5/155) | 5 years | 735 ILCS 5/13-205 | From unreasonable denial |
| Consumer fraud (815 ILCS 505) | 5 years | 735 ILCS 5/13-205 | From discovery of fraud |
| IDOI complaint | No SOL | Administrative | Can file anytime |
| Unfair practice (215 ILCS 5/154.6) | 5 years | 735 ILCS 5/13-205 | Regulatory or private action |

**Key Rule:** The statute of limitations for challenging an improper endorsement
runs from when the carrier first relies on the endorsement to deny or reduce the
claim — NOT from when the endorsement was added. This is because the "injury"
occurs when the endorsement is invoked, not when it is created.

**Exception:** If the endorsement was concealed, the discovery rule may extend the
SOL from when the insured reasonably discovered the endorsement.

## IDOI Complaint Filing

**Illinois Department of Insurance**
- Online: https://idoi.illinois.gov/consumers/file-a-complaint.html
- Phone: (217) 782-4515
- Mail: 320 W. Washington St., Springfield, IL 62767

**What to Include:**
1. Policy number and claim number
2. Copy of original policy (at date of loss)
3. Copy of current policy showing endorsement
4. Endorsement effective date vs. date of loss
5. Evidence of notice (or non-notice) to insured
6. Carrier correspondence citing the endorsement
7. Specific statute violations (215 ILCS 5/143.1, 5/154.6, 50 Ill. Adm. Code 919)

**IDOI Response Timeline:**
- Acknowledgment: 10 business days
- Initial review: 30 days
- Full investigation: 90-120 days
- Carrier response required: Yes, under IDOI supervision
