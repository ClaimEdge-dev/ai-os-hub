## Examples

### Example 1: Residential Hail Claim — State Farm HO-3 (Naperville, IL)

**Background:** John and Mary Smith's Naperville home sustained hail damage on March 15, 2025. State Farm's adjuster offered $4,200 for "minor repairs." ClaimEdge was retained to review the policy and build a coverage strategy.

**Policy Type:** HO-3 (ISO HO 00 03 10/00)

**Coverage Limits Found:**
- Coverage A: $425,000 (RCV)
- Coverage B: $42,500 (RCV)
- Coverage C: $212,500 (ACV — RCV available via endorsement not purchased)
- Coverage D: Unlimited actual expenses
- Deductible: $2,500 flat
- Ordinance & Law: Limited Coverage endorsement OL 01 01 (10% of A = $42,500 per limit)

**Key Findings:**

1. **Underinsurance on Coverage A:** The $425,000 limit was approximately 73.9% of estimated replacement cost ($575,000). This triggered the 80% coinsurance requirement, exposing the insured to an estimated $42,500 penalty. However, because Illinois law (215 ILCS 5/154.6) requires carriers to provide guidance on adequate limits, and State Farm had not recommended a limit increase in 5 years of renewals, a coinsurance waiver argument was viable.

2. **O&L Coverage Adequacy:** The $127,500 total O&L coverage (3 x $42,500) was adequate for the estimated $79,500 in code upgrades. All six identified upgrades were mandated by IRC 2021, IECC 2021, or IBC 2021 as adopted by Naperville/DuPage County.

3. **Carrier's Wear and Tear Exclusion — REJECTED:** The adjuster's attempt to apply the wear and tear exclusion to a 12-year-old roof was rebutted through five independent arguments:
   - Contra proferentem (Cramer v. Insurance Exchange Agency)
   - Reasonable expectations (Wald v. Crusader Ins. Co.)
   - Unfair claims practices (215 ILCS 5/154.6)
   - PA 103-0510 pro-policyholder protections
   - Agent estoppel (text message from agent saying "you're fully covered for hail")

4. **Coverage Gap:** Total gap of $167,650 (19.8%) due to:
   - Coverage A underinsurance: $60,000 gap + $42,500 coinsurance penalty
   - Coverage B inadequacy: $25,000 gap
   - Coverage C ACV gap: $35,000
   - O&L was adequate (no gap)

**Outcome:** The Coverage Opinion Memo was delivered to the client after Robert's review. A formal demand letter was sent to State Farm with all supporting documentation. State Farm reversed its denial within 10 business days and issued a revised estimate of $187,500 — representing a 4,364% increase from the original $4,200 offer. The underinsurance issue was separately pursued against the agent.

### Example 2: Commercial Wind Claim — Liberty Mutual CPP (Des Plaines, IL)

**Background:** A commercial warehouse in Des Plaines sustained wind damage to its R-panel metal roof on June 22, 2025. Liberty Mutual's commercial adjuster denied coverage for the full roof replacement, arguing that only the directly damaged panels needed repair. ClaimEdge was retained.

**Policy Type:** Commercial Package Policy (CPP) — Building & Personal Property Coverage Form (CP 00 10)

**Coverage Limits Found:**
- Building Coverage: $2,400,000 (RCV)
- Business Personal Property: $850,000 (RCV)
- Business Income: $500,000 (Actual Loss Sustained)
- Ordinance & Law: $240,000 (10% of building limit, all three limits)
- Deductible: $10,000 flat
- No coinsurance (agreed value endorsement in effect)

**Key Findings:**

1. **Wind Coverage Confirmed:** The policy explicitly covered "windstorm or hail" as a covered cause of loss under the Building coverage. The loss was unequivocally covered.

2. **Matching Issues — Building Uniformity:** The carrier's position that only 30% of panels needed replacement was rebutted under IBC 2021 Section 3403.1 (repair materials must match existing) and the "matching" or "uniform appearance" doctrine. Because the 20-year-old R-panel profile was no longer manufactured, replacement of the entire roof surface was required for uniform appearance.

3. **Code Upgrade Triggers:**
   - IECC 2021 C402.1: Roof insulation R-value upgrade from R-15 to R-30 ($45,000)
   - IBC 2021 1507.2.1: Underlayment upgrade to self-adhering ice barrier ($18,000)
   - IBC 2021 3403.1: Full panel replacement for uniformity ($120,000)

4. **O&L Adequacy:** The $240,000 total O&L was more than adequate for the $183,000 in code upgrades.

5. **Coverage Gap:** None — the $2,400,000 building limit was approximately 95% of estimated replacement cost, well above the 80% threshold.

**Argument Strategy:**
- Primary: IBC 2021 matching requirements (technical, irrefutable)
- Secondary: Liberty Mutual's own claim handling guidelines require matching assessment
- Tertiary: 215 ILCS 5/154.6 — carrier's failure to investigate matching requirements

**Outcome:** Liberty Mutual accepted the full scope after ClaimEdge provided the IBC citations and matching analysis. Total claim settlement: $847,500 (building + BPP + BI + O&L).

### Example 3: Residential Water Claim — Allstate HO-5 (Oak Lawn, IL)

**Background:** A Oak Lawn homeowner discovered water damage in their basement ceiling on July 3, 2025. The source was traced to a leaking dishwasher supply line on the main floor. Allstate denied coverage, citing the "water damage" exclusion for "water below the surface of the ground." ClaimEdge was retained.

**Policy Type:** HO-5 (ISO HO 00 05 10/00) — Comprehensive Form

**Coverage Limits Found:**
- Coverage A: $380,000 (RCV)
- Coverage B: $38,000 (RCV)
- Coverage C: $190,000 (RCV — HO-5 provides RCV for contents)
- Coverage D: $114,000 (20% of A — HO-5 enhancement)
- Water Backup: Endorsement purchased — $25,000 limit
- Deductible: $1,500 flat

**Key Findings:**

1. **Coverage Denial Was WRONG:** Allstate's adjuster misapplied the "water below the surface of the ground" exclusion. The water came from an interior plumbing supply line — not groundwater, not seepage, not sewer backup. The "surface water" and "below ground" exclusions do not apply to interior plumbing failures.

2. **The Real Exclusion — Sudden and Accidental:** The correct analysis is that interior plumbing leaks are COVERED under the HO-5 "risk of direct physical loss" unless excluded. The applicable exclusion is "continuous or repeated seepage or leakage of water" over 14+ days. If the leak was sudden (supply line rupture), it IS covered. Only gradual seepage over 14+ days is excluded.

3. **Water Backup Endorsement — NOT NEEDED:** Because the water came from a supply line (pressurized plumbing), not a drain/sewer/sump, the Water Backup endorsement was not the relevant coverage. The loss fell under the base policy's plumbing coverage.

4. **Bad Faith Indicators:**
   - Adjuster did not interview the insured about when the leak started
   - No moisture meter readings taken
   - No attempt to distinguish sudden vs. gradual leak
   - Denial issued 48 hours after inspection with no coverage analysis letter
   - Incorrect exclusion cited (surface water instead of seepage)

**Argument Strategy:**
- Primary: Wrong exclusion cited — water was from interior plumbing, not groundwater (215 ILCS 5/154.6(g) — reasonable investigation required)
- Secondary: Even if seepage exclusion applies, it requires 14+ days — no evidence timeline was investigated
- Tertiary: Bad faith under 215 ILCS 5/155 — denial without reasonable investigation
- PA 103-0510: Carrier denied based on wrong exclusion = technical denial prohibited

**Coverage Gap:** No underinsurance. The $380,000 Coverage A was approximately 88% of estimated replacement cost. Estimated water damage: $42,000 ( Coverage A well within limits).

**Outcome:** After ClaimEdge sent the Coverage Opinion Memo pointing out the wrong exclusion, Allstate re-opened the claim, assigned a new adjuster, and paid the full $42,000 plus ALE of $8,200. A separate bad faith claim was pursued under 215 ILCS 5/155 for the wrongful denial, resulting in an additional $15,000 statutory penalty.

## Error Handling

### Error 1: Missing Policy Documents

**Condition:** Policy documents uploaded are incomplete — missing policy form, endorsements, or declarations page.

**Resolution:**
1. Flag missing documents in review status
2. Request missing documents from client
3. If client doesn't have copies: request from carrier (insured has right to complete policy copy)
4. If carrier refuses: cite 215 ILCS 5/143.13a — insurer must provide readable copy of policy
5. Proceed with partial review using available documents; flag limitations

**Output:** `review_status: "incomplete_pending_documents"` with list of missing items.

---

### Error 2: Unreadable/OCR-Failed Documents

**Condition:** Policy documents are poor-quality scans or handwritten amendments that cannot be read via OCR.

**Resolution:**
1. Attempt OCR with multiple engines
2. If OCR fails: flag for manual transcription
3. Prompt user to provide clearer scans or retype key sections
4. For handwritten amendments: flag as "requires manual review"
5. Proceed with best-effort extraction; note confidence level

**Output:** `extraction_confidence: <0.70` with validation_notes listing unreadable sections.

---

### Error 3: Out-of-State Policy

**Condition:** Policy was issued in a state other than Illinois.

**Resolution:**
1. Flag out-of-state status
2. Load applicable state insurance code instead of Illinois statutes
3. Adjust all legal citations to the correct state's law
4. Note that ClaimEdge primarily operates under Illinois law
5. Recommend local counsel if significant legal differences exist
6. Continue analysis with appropriate state law

**Output:** Policy review proceeds with `state_of_issue` flag and adjusted legal framework.

---

### Error 4: Ambiguous Policy Language

**Condition:** Policy language is unclear, contradictory, or subject to multiple reasonable interpretations.

**Resolution:**
1. Document all possible interpretations
2. Apply contra proferentem — favor interpretation favoring coverage
3. Research case law for judicial interpretation of same/similar language
4. Flag for Robert's judgment call
5. Build arguments for all favorable interpretations
6. Note that ambiguity itself supports coverage under Illinois law

**Output:** Multiple argument paths documented; flagged for human review.

---

### Error 5: Carrier Denies Coverage During Review

**Condition:** While the policy review is being conducted, the carrier issues a coverage denial.

**Resolution:**
1. Immediately escalate to Robert
2. Analyze denial letter against policy language
3. Identify if correct exclusion was cited
4. Begin Coverage Argument Builder (Function 5) immediately
5. Document timeline — bad faith clock starts at denial
6. Prepare IDOI complaint template if denial is unlawful
7. Do NOT stop the policy review — complete it to identify all coverage arguments

**Output:** Coverage review continues with `carrier_denial_received: true` flag and accelerated timeline.

---

### Error 6: Estimated Damages Exceed All Coverage

**Condition:** Total estimated damages exceed total available coverage by a significant margin.

**Resolution:**
1. Calculate total gap and gap percentage
2. Identify underinsurance and coinsurance issues
3. Check for umbrella policy or excess coverage
4. Evaluate agent E&O claim for failure to recommend adequate limits
5. Recommend attorney consultation immediately
6. Document coverage inadequacy for potential bad faith claim

**Output:** `escalation_needed: true` with attorney recommendation and E&O analysis.
