## Primary Statutes

### 215 ILCS 5/155 — Unreasonable Delay or Denial (Bad Faith)

**Statutory Text:**
> "In any action by or against a company wherein there is in issue the liability of a company on a policy or contract of insurance or the amount of the loss payable thereunder, or for an unreasonable delay in settling a claim, and it appears from the evidence that the company has refused to pay the full amount of a claim or portion thereof which it is obligated to pay under the policy or contract of insurance, or has otherwise delayed payment of the claim, and that such refusal or delay was vexatious and unreasonable, the court may allow as part of the judgment... interest on the amount of the claim... plus attorneys' fees and costs."

**Key Elements:**
1. Company has obligation to pay under policy
2. Company refused or delayed payment
3. Refusal or delay was vexatious and unreasonable
4. Court MAY award: 9% prejudgment interest + attorney fees + costs + emotional distress + actual damages

**Application to Policy Review:**
- If carrier denies coverage for clearly covered element → bad faith candidate
- Document the clear coverage → carrier denial timeline
- 9% interest on full claim amount from date of denial

### 215 ILCS 5/154.6 — Claim Handling Standards

**Statutory Text:**
> "(a) Within 15 working days after receipt by the company of notice of a claim, the company shall: (1) acknowledge the receipt of such notice; (2) promptly provide the claimant with necessary claim forms, instructions, and reasonable assistance so that the claimant can comply with the policy conditions and the company's reasonable requirements; and (3) begin any investigation of the claim.

> (b) Within 45 working days after receipt by the company of properly executed proof of loss forms, the company shall inform the claimant in writing of the acceptance or denial of the claim.

> (c) No insurer shall refuse to pay a claim on the basis of the insured's failure to submit a preliminary claim report or a formal proof of loss within a specified time unless the insurer can demonstrate that the failure has substantially prejudiced the insurer's rights."

**Key Timelines:**
- 15 working days: Acknowledge + begin investigation
- 45 working days: Accept or deny decision
- Insured's late notice not a bar unless carrier prejudiced

**Application to Policy Review:**
- If carrier misses 45-day deadline → bad faith indicator
- If carrier demands unnecessary documentation → violation
- Late notice argument must show prejudice — if no prejudice, coverage stands

### 215 ILCS 5/143.1 — Duty to Defend

**Key Principle:**
- Illinois: duty to defend is broader than duty to indemnify
- If claim COULD potentially fall within coverage → carrier must defend
- Carrier cannot refuse defense until coverage determined

**Application:**
- Third-party claims against insured
- Liability coverage disputes
- Even if claim ultimately not covered → defense costs during litigation

---

### 815 ILCS 505/1 — Consumer Fraud and Deceptive Business Practices Act

**Application:**
- Unfair claims practices may violate Consumer Fraud Act
- Available in addition to 215 ILCS 5/155
- Allows for punitive damages in egregious cases
- Class action potential for pattern of practice

## Recent Amendments

### PA 103-0510 — Matching Requirements (Effective 2024)

**Statutory Text:**
> "If an insurer provides coverage for the repair or replacement of damaged exterior surfaces and matching materials are not reasonably available, the insurer shall provide coverage for the reasonable and necessary repair or replacement of the undamaged exterior surfaces to achieve a reasonable uniform appearance."

**Key Requirements:**
1. Applies to: roofing, siding, windows, doors (exterior surfaces)
2. "Reasonably available" standard — if exact match unavailable → full replacement
3. "Reasonable uniform appearance" — not perfection, but matching
4. Carrier cannot limit to "like kind and quality" only

**Application to Policy Review:**
- Check ALL exterior damage claims for matching issues
- If material discontinued or color changed → full replacement required
- Document: original material, current availability, visual mismatch
- Cite PA 103-0510 in every supplement demanding full replacement

**Enforcement:**
- Violation = unfair claims practice under 215 ILCS 5/154.6
- Pattern of violation = Consumer Fraud Act claim
- File complaint with Illinois Department of Insurance

---

### PA 103-0509 — Scope of Coverage for Cosmetic Damage (Related)

**Key Provision:**
- Clarifies that cosmetic damage to exterior surfaces IS damage
- Cannot be excluded merely because it doesn't affect "function"
- Aesthetic value is part of property value

**Application:**
- Counter cosmetic damage exclusions
- Argue that appearance is part of property function
- Cite alongside PA 103-0510 for full replacement

## Regulatory Guidance

### Illinois Department of Insurance Bulletins

**Bulletin 2015-05: Claims Handling Practices**
- Prohibits requiring insured to obtain multiple estimates
- Prohibits delaying claim pending unreasonable documentation
- Requires timely payment of undisputed amounts

**Bulletin 2017-03: Matching of Exterior Building Materials**
- Predecessor to PA 103-0510
- Encouraged carriers to match materials when feasible
- Now superseded by statute but shows regulatory intent

**Bulletin 2020-01: COVID-19 and Business Interruption**
- Relevant for commercial policy review
- Clarified that virus exclusion must be specific

## Coverage Interpretation Doctrines

### 1. Contra Proferentem
- Ambiguous policy language construed against drafter (carrier)
- Illinois: strong doctrine, heavily favors insured
- Application: Any coverage dispute where language has two meanings

### 2. Reasonable Expectations Doctrine
- Coverage based on what insured reasonably expected
- Even if policy language technically excludes
- Illinois recognizes but applies cautiously
- Application: When marketing materials promised broader coverage

### 3. Efficient Proximate Cause
- When multiple causes combine, the EFFICIENT cause controls
- If covered peril set chain in motion → coverage
- Illinois: applies to all concurrent causation scenarios

### 4. Fortuity Requirement
- Insurance covers fortuitous losses, not intentional or inevitable
- Normal wear and tear = not fortuitous → excluded
- Sudden damage = fortuitous → covered

### 5. Fourth Estate Doctrine
- Policy interpreted as a whole, not in isolated provisions
- Exclusions read in context of entire policy
- Illinois: all provisions must be harmonized if possible
