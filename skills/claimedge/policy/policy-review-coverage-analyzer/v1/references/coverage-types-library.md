## 1. Coverage A — Dwelling/Building

### Standard Limits
- Typically stated as a dollar amount on dec page
- Some policies use replacement cost estimator (Marshall & Swift, etc.)
- Coinsurance: 80%, 90%, or 100% (commercial); none (most HO with guaranteed replacement)

### Key Illinois Issues

**Underinsurance:**
- Illinois does not require agents to verify replacement cost accuracy
- Agent E&O may apply if coverage clearly inadequate
- 215 ILCS 5/154.6 requires carriers to act in good faith on valuation

**Functional Replacement Cost (FRC):**
- Common on older homes in Chicago area
- Replaces with functionally equivalent materials, not exact match
- Affects matching arguments for siding/roofing

**Guaranteed Replacement Cost vs. Extended Replacement Cost:**
- Guaranteed: pays full replacement regardless of limit (rare)
- Extended: 125-150% of Coverage A limit (more common)
- Verify which applies — affects total recoverable

### Documentation Checklist
- [ ] Coverage A limit stated on dec page
- [ ] Coinsurance percentage (if commercial)
- [ ] Guaranteed or extended replacement cost endorsement
- [ ] Inflation guard percentage
- [ ] Recent appraisal or valuation method
- [ ] Age of dwelling (affects FRC)

## 2. Coverage B — Other Structures

### Standard Limits
- HO policies: 10% of Coverage A (standard), can be increased
- DP policies: 10% of Coverage A
- Commercial: separately stated limit

### What Qualifies as "Other Structure"
- Detached garage, shed, fence, gazebo, pergola
- MUST be on insured premises and separated by clear space
- Attached structures are part of Coverage A

### Key Illinois Issues

**Detached Garages:**
- Very common in Chicagoland (alley garages)
- Often have different siding/roofing than main dwelling
- Matching issues under PA 103-0510

**Fences:**
- Covered under B but with sub-limits on some policies
- Check if ornamental fence has separate limit

### Documentation Checklist
- [ ] Coverage B limit
- [ ] List of detached structures on property
- [ ] Construction materials of each (for matching)
- [ ] Age of each structure

## 3. Coverage C — Personal Property

### Standard Limits
- HO-3: 50% of Coverage A (standard), can increase to 70%
- HO-5: 70% of Coverage A (standard)
- DP: 0% unless landlord's furnishings endorsement
- Commercial: separately stated

### Valuation Methods
- **RCV (Replacement Cost Value):** Pays cost to replace new — requires HO 04 90 endorsement on contents
- **ACV (Actual Cash Value):** Pays depreciated value — standard on named peril contents
- **Agreed Value:** Scheduled items at agreed amounts

### Special Limits (Common)
See Phase 2 special limits table in policy-analysis-framework.md

### Key Illinois Issues

**Contents in Detached Garage:**
- Covered under Coverage C (not B)
- Off-premises limit (typically 10% of C) may apply
- Tools and equipment may have business property sub-limit

**Tenant's Contents (Rental Properties):**
- DP policies do NOT cover tenant contents
- Tenant needs renter's policy (HO-4)
- Landlord's personal property in unit: covered under landlord's furnishings endorsement

### Documentation Checklist
- [ ] Coverage C limit
- [ ] RCV vs. ACV valuation
- [ ] Special limits inventory
- [ ] High-value items needing scheduling
- [ ] Contents in detached structures

## 4. Coverage D — Loss of Use / ALE

### Standard Limits
- HO policies: 20-30% of Coverage A, typically NO time limit
- DP policies: Fair Rental Value (for rental properties), no time limit
- Commercial: Business Income coverage with waiting period and limit

### What ALE Covers
- Temporary housing (hotel, rental home)
- Additional food costs (restaurants vs. groceries)
- Storage fees
- Laundry costs
- Transportation differences
- Pet boarding
- Moving costs

### What ALE Does NOT Cover
- Mortgage payments (continue regardless)
- Utility bills at damaged home (may be reduced)
- Normal living expenses (only ADDITIONAL costs)

### Key Illinois Issues

**Duration:**
- Illinois standard HO policies typically have NO time limit on ALE
- Limit is dollar-based only
- Check policy language carefully — some endorsements add time limits

**Rental Properties:**
- Fair Rental Value covers lost rent while uninhabitable
- Requires proof of rental income (tax returns, lease)
- Ends when unit is habitable, not when fully repaired

**Commercial Business Income:**
- Waiting period: typically 24-72 hours
- Coverage period: restoration period + extended period (typically 30 days)
- Covers net income + continuing operating expenses

### Documentation Checklist
- [ ] Coverage D limit and type
- [ ] Time limit (if any)
- [ ] Client's temporary housing situation
- [ ] Rental income documentation (if applicable)
- [ ] Business income documentation (if commercial)

## 5. Ordinance or Law

### Standard Limits
- HO policies: 10% of Coverage A (standard)
- Can be increased to 25% or 50% via endorsement
- Commercial: separately stated, often higher

### Three Coverage Components
1. **Coverage A:** Demolition of undamaged portion (when required by ordinance)
2. **Coverage B:** Increased cost of reconstruction to meet current codes
3. **Coverage C:** Demolition and removal of debris from undamaged portion

### Key Illinois Issues

**Code Upgrade Triggers (IRC 2021 / IBC 2021 / IECC 2021):**
- When > 25% of roof area is damaged → full roof replacement required
- Spaced decking → solid decking required (IRC R905.2.1)
- Drip edge required (IRC R905.2.8.5)
- Ice barrier required (IRC R905.1.2) — critical for Illinois
- Valley lining required
- Ventilation requirements

**PA 103-0510 — Matching Requirements:**
- Requires reasonable uniform appearance
- If matching materials unavailable, full replacement required
- Applies to roofing, siding, windows, doors

**Common O&L Arguments:**
- Spaced decking replacement → solid decking
- 3-tab shingles → architectural (if 3-tab discontinued)
- Aluminum siding → vinyl (if aluminum discontinued)
- Insufficient insulation → code-required R-value

### Documentation Checklist
- [ ] O&L limit and % of Coverage A
- [ ] Which 3 coverages (A/B/C) are included
- [ ] Municipal building department contact
- [ ] Adopted code year for jurisdiction
- [ ] Local amendments to IRC/IBC

## 6. Water/Sewer Backup

### Standard Coverage
- NOT included in standard HO-3
- Requires endorsement: HO 04 95 (Water Backup and Sump Discharge)
- Typical limit: $5,000-$25,000
- Separate deductible may apply

### What It Covers
- Water that backs up through sewers or drains
- Water that overflows from sump pump
- Water that enters through basement wall cracks from underground sources

### What It Does NOT Cover
- Flood (requires separate flood policy)
- Surface water
- Water from above-ground sources
- Gradual seepage

### Key Illinois Issues

**Combined Sewer Overflows (CSO):**
- Common in older Chicago-area municipalities
- Heavy rain overwhelms combined sewer system
- Backup through floor drains is covered with endorsement

**Sump Pump Failure:**
- Power outage during storm = common scenario
- Covered if sump pump endorsement in place
- Not covered if no endorsement (argue as plumbing overflow?)

**Foundation Seepage:**
- Generally excluded as "below surface water"
- Some policies have foundation endorsement
- Illinois case law may support coverage if seepage is sudden

### Documentation Checklist
- [ ] Water backup endorsement present? (Yes/No)
- [ ] Limit and deductible
- [ ] Type of water loss (backup vs. overflow vs. seepage)
- [ ] Sump pump age and maintenance history
- [ ] Municipal sewer type (combined vs. separate)

## 7. Foundation/Water Damage

### Standard Coverage
- NOT included in standard HO-3
- Some carriers offer foundation endorsement
- Covers water damage to foundation from underground sources

### What It Covers
- Water infiltration through foundation walls
- Hydrostatic pressure damage
- Foundation wall cracking from water pressure

### What It Does NOT Cover
- Settling/cracking from normal soil conditions
- Poor construction
- Maintenance issues

### Key Illinois Issues

**Clay Soil Expansion:**
- Common in Illinois (particularly Chicago area)
- Seasonal expansion/contraction causes foundation movement
- Generally excluded as "earth movement" or settling

**Basement Water in New Construction:**
- Illinois requires waterproofing on new foundations
- If waterproofing fails, may be construction defect (not covered)
- May have builder's warranty claim

### Documentation Checklist
- [ ] Foundation endorsement present? (Yes/No)
- [ ] Type of foundation damage
- [ ] Age of foundation
- [ ] Previous water history
- [ ] Drainage conditions around property

## 8. Mold/Fungus Coverage

### Standard Coverage
- Limited coverage in standard HO-3: typically $10,000
- Can be increased via endorsement
- Some carriers exclude entirely or limit to $5,000
- Requires endorsement for higher limits

### What Standard Coverage Includes
- Testing for mold
- Remediation of mold
- Removal of mold-damaged materials
- Reconstruction after mold removal

### Key Illinois Issues

**Health Concerns:**
- Illinois does not have specific mold disclosure laws for insurance
- Stachybotrys (black mold) common in water-damage claims
- Remediation must follow EPA/IICRC standards

**Coverage Arguments:**
- If mold resulted from covered water loss → covered (within limits)
- If mold from excluded cause (flood, seepage) → not covered
- Document chain: covered peril → water damage → mold growth

### Documentation Checklist
- [ ] Mold coverage limit
- [ ] Type of mold (if identified)
- [ ] Cause of mold growth
- [ ] Remediation estimate
- [ ] Health concerns for occupants

## 9. Debris Removal

### Standard Coverage
- Included in standard HO-3 as additional coverage
- Typically $[amount] additional beyond Coverage A limit
- Some policies: up to 25% of Coverage A

### What It Covers
- Removal of damaged building materials
- Disposal fees
- Additional coverage if debris removal exhausts Coverage A

### What It Does NOT Cover
- Removal of undamaged materials (unless O&L applies)
- Hazardous material disposal (asbestos, lead — may need separate coverage)

### Key Illinois Issues

**Asbestos/Legal Hazardous Materials:**
- Pre-1980 homes likely have asbestos-containing materials
- Illinois requires licensed abatement contractors
- Disposal costs significantly higher than normal debris
- Check if policy has pollution/hazardous material coverage

**Landfill Fees:**
- Illinois landfill rates vary by county
- Some policies cap debris removal separately from disposal

### Documentation Checklist
- [ ] Debris removal limit
- [ ] Type of debris (construction materials, contents, hazardous)
- [ ] Asbestos/lead testing results (if applicable)
- [ ] Disposal estimates

## 10. Trees/Shrubs/Landscaping

### Standard Coverage
- Included in standard HO-3: $500 per tree, $[total] aggregate
- Covers damage from named perils (fire, lightning, vandalism, vehicle, aircraft)
- Does NOT cover wind/hail/tree fall (unless strikes insured building)

### What It Covers
- Removal of fallen trees that hit insured building
- Replacement of damaged trees/shrubs
- Usually limited to trees on insured premises

### Key Illinois Issues

**Tree Fall on Structure:**
- If neighbor's tree falls on insured building → covered (subject to deductible)
- If insured's tree falls on neighbor's building → liability coverage may apply
- If tree falls on ground only → no coverage unless blocks driveway

**Emerald Ash Borer:**
- Widespread in Illinois
- Tree death from EAB is NOT covered (pest damage excluded)
- If EAB-weakened tree falls on house → coverage may apply for structure damage

### Documentation Checklist
- [ ] Tree/shrub limit per item and aggregate
- [ ] Number and type of damaged trees
- [ ] Cause of damage (peril that caused it)
- [ ] Whether tree hit insured structure

## 11. Service Line Coverage

### Standard Coverage
- NOT included in standard HO-3
- Requires endorsement (varies by carrier)
- Covers underground service lines from street to house

### What It Covers
- Water supply lines
- Sewer lines
- Gas lines
- Electric lines
- Cable/communication lines

### What It Does NOT Cover
- Lines on other people's property
- Septic systems (may have separate coverage)
- Wells

### Key Illinois Issues

**Sewer Line Responsibility:**
- In Illinois, homeowner typically responsible for sewer line from house to street connection
- Tree root intrusion is common cause
- Excavation costs can exceed $10,000

**Water Main Breaks:**
- Municipal water main break can damage private service line
- If municipality at fault → may have municipal liability claim

### Documentation Checklist
- [ ] Service line endorsement present? (Yes/No)
- [ ] Limit and deductible
- [ ] Type of line damaged
- [ ] Location of break/damage
- [ ] Excavation estimate

## 12. Equipment Breakdown

### Standard Coverage
- NOT included in standard HO-3
- Requires endorsement (formerly called boiler & machinery)
- Covers mechanical/electrical breakdown of equipment

### What It Covers
- HVAC systems
- Water heaters
- Electrical panels
- Generators
- Sump pumps
- Appliances
- Smart home systems

### What It Does NOT Cover
- Normal wear and tear
- Rust/corrosion
- Maintenance issues
- Manufacturer defect (may have warranty claim)

### Key Illinois Issues

**HVAC Failures:**
- Common in extreme weather (Illinois summers and winters)
- Compressor failure = expensive ($3,000-$7,000)
- Check if failure caused by covered peril (power surge, lightning)

**Power Surge Damage:**
- Illinois summer storms cause frequent power surges
- Standard HO-3 covers "artificially generated electrical current"
- Utility company may be liable if surge originated off-premises

### Documentation Checklist
- [ ] Equipment breakdown endorsement present? (Yes/No)
- [ ] Limit and deductible
- [ ] Equipment that failed
- [ ] Cause of failure
- [ ] Age and maintenance history
- [ ] Repair/replacement estimate

## 13. Loss Assessment

### Standard Coverage
- NOT included in standard HO-3
- Requires endorsement: HO 04 20
- Covers assessments by HOA or condominium association

### What It Covers
- Special assessments for common area damage
- Deductible assessments (HOA master policy deductible)
- Liability assessments

### What It Does NOT Cover
- Regular HOA dues
- Assessments for maintenance
- Assessments you would have paid anyway

### Key Illinois Issues

**Condo Master Policy Deductibles:**
- Illinois condo associations can assess unit owners for master policy deductibles
- Can be $10,000-$50,000+
- Loss assessment endorsement critical for condo owners

**Townhome/Planned Community:**
- Shared structures (retaining walls, entry monuments)
- Storm damage to shared structures may result in assessment

### Documentation Checklist
- [ ] Loss assessment endorsement present? (Yes/No)
- [ ] Limit (typically $1,000-$50,000)
- [ ] HOA/association name and contact
- [ ] Master policy information
- [ ] Any assessment notices received

## 14. Liability Coverages

### Coverage E — Personal Liability
- Standard limit: $100,000 (can increase to $500,000 or $1M)
- Covers: bodily injury and property damage to others
- Legal defense costs: typically in addition to limit

### Coverage F — Medical Payments
- Standard limit: $1,000 per person (can increase)
- Covers: medical expenses for guests injured on premises
- No-fault coverage (does not require negligence)

### Key Illinois Issues

**Dog Bite Claims:**
- Illinois has strict liability for dog bites (510 ILCS 5/16)
- Homeowner's liability covers dog bites
- Some carriers exclude specific breeds
- Check for animal liability endorsement

**Swimming Pool Liability:**
- Illinois requires specific safety measures for pools
- Failure to maintain may trigger negligence exclusion
- Check for pool liability endorsement

**Ice/Snow Slip and Fall:**
- Illinois Natural Accumulation Rule: property owner not liable for natural snow/ice
- Artificial accumulation (leaky gutter, poor drainage) → may be liable
- Check policy for premises liability exclusions

**Contractor Injury:**
- If contractor (roofer, plumber) injured on property
- May be covered under liability
- Worker's compensation exclusion may apply if contractor has employees

### Documentation Checklist
- [ ] Coverage E limit
- [ ] Coverage F limit
- [ ] Liability exclusions (animals, pools, business)
- [ ] Any claims history
- [ ] Umbrella policy (if applicable)
