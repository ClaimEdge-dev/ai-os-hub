# Coverage Types Library

## 1. Dwelling Coverage (Coverage A)
**Definition:** Coverage for the physical structure of the home including attached structures.
**Standard Limit:** Replacement cost value (RCV) or actual cash value (ACV)
**Code Upgrade Trigger:** IRC 2021/2024 compliance requirements
**Common Exclusions:**
- Earth movement
- Water damage (flood)
- Ordinance or law (unless endorsed)
- Neglect/failure to maintain
**Dispute Arguments:**
- Full RCV vs ACV: 215 ILCS 5/154.6 requires replacement cost for total losses
- Code upgrade: IRC upgrades are not "betterment" but compliance requirements
- Decking: Full decking replacement required per manufacturer specs

## 2. Other Structures (Coverage B)
**Definition:** Detached structures like garages, sheds, fences.
**Standard Limit:** 10% of Coverage A
**Code Upgrade Trigger:** Same as Coverage A for applicable structures
**Common Exclusions:**
- Structures used for business
- Rented to others
- Under construction
**Dispute Arguments:**
- Garage interiors: Foil-backed drywall, vermiculite insulation
- Separate structures with damage require individual assessment

## 3. Personal Property (Coverage C)
**Definition:** Contents and personal belongings.
**Standard Limit:** 50-70% of Coverage A
**Special Limits:**
- Jewelry: $1,500
- Cash: $200
- Firearms: $2,500
- Silverware: $2,500
**Common Exclusions:**
- Business property
- Motor vehicles
- Animals/birds/fish
**Dispute Arguments:**
- Off-premises coverage extends to temporary living situations
- Replacement cost endorsement overrides ACV depreciation

## 4. Loss of Use (Coverage D)
**Definition:** Additional living expenses when home is uninhabitable.
**Standard Limit:** 20% of Coverage A
**Duration:** Until repairs complete or policy limit reached
**Common Exclusions:**
- Loss due to government action
- Power outage off premises
**Dispute Arguments:**
- "Uninhabitable" is broader than "damaged" - includes unsafe conditions
- Extended stays for code compliance delays
- Storage costs for undamaged personal property

## 5. Ordinance or Law (Code Upgrade)
**Definition:** Additional coverage for code compliance upgrades.
**Standard Limit:** 10-25% of Coverage A (varies by policy)
**Critical Triggers:**
- IRC 2021/2024 decking requirements
- Ventilation code compliance
- Cricket/valley flashing requirements
- WRB (weather-resistant barrier) requirements
**Dispute Arguments:**
- Carrier cannot apply O&L deductible to code-required work
- Full replacement triggers full code compliance obligation
- Local amendments increase code requirements beyond IRC minimum

## 6. Liability Coverage (Coverage E)
**Definition:** Legal liability for bodily injury or property damage to others.
**Standard Limit:** $100,000 - $500,000
**Common Exclusions:**
- Business operations
- Intentional acts
- Motor vehicles
**ClaimEdge Note:** Rarely applicable to property damage claims but may be relevant for contractor disputes.

## 7. Medical Payments (Coverage F)
**Definition:** Medical expenses for guests injured on property.
**Standard Limit:** $1,000 - $5,000
**ClaimEdge Note:** Separate from liability; no fault required.

## 8. Roof Surfaces Extended Coverage
**Definition:** Enhanced coverage for roof damage including matching and cosmetic damage.
**Common Features:**
- Matching siding/shingle provisions
- Cosmetic damage waivers
- Hail-resistant material upgrades
**Dispute Arguments:**
- "Cosmetic" exclusion does not apply to functional damage
- Matching provisions require uniform appearance
- Hail-resistant upgrades are code upgrades, not betterment

## 9. Water Backup/Sump Pump Overflow
**Definition:** Coverage for water damage from backed up drains or sump pump failure.
**Standard Limit:** $5,000 - $25,000
**Common Exclusions:**
- Flood (requires separate policy)
- Continuous/seepage over 14 days
**Dispute Arguments:**
- Sudden vs. gradual: Backed up drain is sudden event
- Resulting damage: Even if cause excluded, resulting mold/structural damage may be covered

## 10. Service Line Coverage
**Definition:** Coverage for underground service lines (water, sewer, electric).
**Standard Limit:** $10,000
**Common Exclusions:**
- Lines not on insured property
- Tree root intrusion (may be covered as collapse)
**Dispute Arguments:**
- Collapse of line triggers coverage even if cause is excluded
- Excavation costs are part of covered repair

## 11. Equipment Breakdown
**Definition:** Coverage for mechanical/electrical equipment failure.
**Standard Limit:** Varies
**Covered Equipment:**
- HVAC systems
- Water heaters
- Electrical panels
- Sump pumps
**Dispute Arguments:**
- Equipment damaged by covered peril (hail, wind) is not "breakdown"
- Resulting water damage from equipment failure may be covered under other provisions

## 12. Mold/Fungus Coverage
**Definition:** Limited coverage for mold remediation.
**Standard Limit:** $10,000 (typically)
**Common Exclusions:**
- Mold from flood
- Long-term moisture
- Preventable conditions
**Dispute Arguments:**
- Mold resulting from covered water damage is covered
- Remediation includes containment, removal, AND reconstruction
- 14-day window applies to discovery, not growth initiation
