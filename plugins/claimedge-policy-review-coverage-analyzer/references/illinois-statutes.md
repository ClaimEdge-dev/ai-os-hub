# Illinois Statutes — Policy Coverage

## Primary Statutes

### 215 ILCS 5/143 — Standard Fire Policy
**Key Provisions:**
- Defines minimum coverage for fire policies
- Requires actual cash value or replacement cost
- Governing statute for fire-related claims

**Application:**
- Foundation for all property coverage analysis
- Minimum standards that cannot be excluded

### 215 ILCS 5/154 — Unfair Discrimination
**Key Provisions:**
- Prohibits unfair discrimination between insureds
- Requires consistent application of policy terms
- Prevents arbitrary denial of coverage

**Application:**
- Challenge when carrier treats similar claims differently
- Argument against selective exclusion application

### 215 ILCS 5/154.6 — Prompt Payment of Claims
**Key Provisions:**
- Requires payment within 15 days of settlement
- Replacement cost must be paid for total losses
- Prevents unreasonable delay in payment

**Critical Language:**
> "In the event of a total loss under a fire policy, the insurer shall pay the full amount of insurance upon such property."

**Application:**
- Foundation for replacement cost arguments
- Triggers bad faith if carrier delays payment
- Requires full RCV, not ACV, for total losses

### 215 ILCS 5/155 — Bad Faith / Vexatious Delay
**Key Provisions:**
- Penalties for vexatious and unreasonable delay
- Attorney fees and 9% interest
- Punitive damages in egregious cases

**Critical Language:**
> "In any action brought against any company... if it shall appear to the court that the company has refused to pay the full amount of a loss... without just cause, the court may award attorney fees and 9% interest."

**Application:**
- Ultimate penalty for unreasonable coverage denial
- Applies when carrier refuses to acknowledge valid coverage
- Must show carrier's position is objectively unreasonable

### 215 ILCS 5/154.5 — Insurer's Duty to Defend
**Key Provisions:**
- Requires insurer to defend insured against claims
- Broader than duty to indemnify
- Applies to liability coverage disputes

### 815 ILCS 505/1 — Consumer Fraud Act
**Key Provisions:**
- Prohibits deceptive business practices
- Applies to insurance sales and claims handling
- Private right of action for consumers

**Application:**
- Challenge misleading policy language
- Argument against deceptive coverage denials
- Treble damages available

## Administrative Regulations

### 50 Ill. Adm. Code Part 919 — Unfair Claims Practices
**Key Provisions:**
- Section 919.50: Prompt investigation required
- Section 919.60: Good faith settlement required
- Section 919.80: Prohibited claims practices

**Prohibited Practices:**
- Refusing to pay claims without reasonable basis
- Failing to affirm or deny coverage within reasonable time
- Offering substantially less than amount due
- Failing to provide reasonable explanation for denial

**Application:**
- Filing IDOI complaint against carrier
- Supporting bad faith claim under 215 ILCS 5/155
- Evidence of systematic claims mishandling

### 50 Ill. Adm. Code Section 919.80(k)
**Critical Language:**
> "Failing to provide a reasonable basis in the insurance policy or applicable law for a denial of a claim."

**Application:**
- Direct prohibition against unsupported exclusion application
- Foundation for IDOI complaint
- Evidence of bad faith when carrier cannot cite specific policy language

## Case Law

### Coverage Interpretation

#### Hamel v. American Family (Ill. App. 2005)
**Holding:** Code compliance upgrades are part of repair obligation, not betterment.
**Application:** Ordinance or Law coverage disputes
**Key Quote:** "Building code compliance is not an improvement but a requirement of lawful repair."

#### Paradise v. Nowatzke (Ill. App. 2007)
**Holding:** Replacement cost coverage includes code-compliant materials.
**Application:** RCV vs. ACV with code upgrades
**Key Quote:** "Replacement cost means the cost to replace with materials of like kind and quality that comply with current building codes."

#### Cincinnati v. Motorists (Ill. 1995)
**Holding:** Policy ambiguities construed against drafter (contra proferentem).
**Application:** All ambiguous exclusion language
**Key Quote:** "Any ambiguity in an insurance policy is construed most strongly against the insurer who drafted it."

#### Adams v. Allstate (Ill. App. 2009)
**Holding:** Resulting damage from covered peril is covered even if cause has exclusion.
**Application:** Resulting damage doctrine
**Key Quote:** "The exclusion of the cause does not exclude the effect when the effect is a separate, distinct loss."

### Bad Faith

#### Cramer v. State Farm (Ill. App. 2012)
**Holding:** Carrier must prove exclusion applies; mere possibility insufficient.
**Application:** Burden of proof on exclusions
**Key Quote:** "The insurer bears the burden of proving that an exclusion applies to defeat coverage."

#### Dunn v. State Farm (Ill. 2014)
**Holding:** Pattern of unreasonable denials supports punitive damages.
**Application:** Bad faith under 215 ILCS 5/155
**Key Quote:** "A pattern of denying valid claims without reasonable basis supports an inference of bad faith."

### Prompt Payment

#### Country Preferred v. DeMarcus (Ill. App. 2018)
**Holding:** 15-day payment requirement is mandatory, not directory.
**Application:** Prompt payment violations
**Key Quote:** "The 15-day payment requirement is mandatory. Delay beyond this period without just cause triggers statutory penalties."

## Statute of Limitations

### Contract Claims
- **Standard:** 10 years (735 ILCS 5/13-206)
- **Oral:** 5 years (735 ILCS 5/13-205)

### Bad Faith Claims (215 ILCS 5/155)
- **Standard:** 5 years from denial
- **Discovery Rule:** Starts when insured discovers bad faith

### Consumer Fraud (815 ILCS 505/1)
- **Standard:** 5 years
- **Discovery Rule:** Starts when deception discovered

### IDOI Complaint
- **No statute of limitations**
- File at any time after violation
